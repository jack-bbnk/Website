Resident Evil Village, to me, felt like it focused a little *too* hard on the action over the horror that I enjoy so much in the series. With Biohazard, Resident Evil felt like it had found its footing again with a game that, at times, genuinely felt a little tense. This tension is completely evaporated in Village, the game instead pitting you against more and more enemies in an action-based survival experience. It wasn't bad, by any means - far from it, but I suppose it is not what I want from Resident Evil.

The game starts with a great hook that is maintained well in its first two hours that covers castle Dimitrescu, but fell apart afterward, and has a largely unsatisying payoff bar a couple parts of the ending that I obviously cannot mention for spoiler reasons. To me, and I am aware this is a very unpopular opinion, once you leave the castle, the game becomes much less interesting and starts to instead feel like a means to an end, and I often just pushed through sections for the sake of getting the game done - which is obviously not what you want from a game.

The combat, while improved from Resident Evil 7, still felt quite bare bones and weightless: shooting and/or hitting enemies didn't feel satisfying, and I often couldn't tell if an enemy of boss was dead, as it all felt rather unceremonious or played down. Speaking of, boss battles were all *okay*, and none of them posed a particular threat (besides the last), and none felt at all memorable.

Area design felt VERY linear, and there was a distinct lack of the creative, carefully crafted backtracking puzzle design that I love so much from the series. The puzzles themselves were also just non-present, with the only area in the game having that kind of identity again being castle Dimitrescu. That being said, there was one area of the game I enjoyed above all else and raises my opinion of it much higher - the 'Baby' section. I was almost scared playing it (FINALLY!), feeling a little tense with its claustrophobic, dark, well designed area that had a puzzle-oriented focus and some phenomenal sound design. Its only flaw is that it was SO short, and the threat only appeared two or three times throughout.

Despite the game not really being what I love about Resident Evil, it wasn't a bad game at all. Sure, the gunplay felt a little weak despite the movement improvements, but the game wasn't trying to be something it wasn't, and that identity it strived for was pulled off relatively effectively. It definitely didn't overstay its welcome, but it was easily on the shorter side, only taking me around 6 hours to finish. Way too short for a previously full priced game regardless of its replay value, but for the very cheap price it goes for now, it's a solid bit of fun - but didn't really feel like much more.

*Played on a PS5 on Performance Mode*
